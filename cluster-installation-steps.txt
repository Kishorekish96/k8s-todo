Breaking down your FQDN:
backend-service: The name you gave your service in your backend.yaml file.

default: The Namespace. Since you didn't specify a different namespace in your metadata, Kubernetes automatically put it in the "default" one.

svc: Short for "service," indicating this is a Service resource.

cluster.local: The default domain name for the entire cluster.

Why this is useful for you:
Since you are a Golang developer moving into DevOps, understanding this is key for microservices:

Communication within the same Namespace: If your frontend pod and backend pod are both in the default namespace, the frontend can simply use http://backend-service:9000. Kubernetes is smart enough to fill in the rest.

Communication across Namespaces: If you later move your database to a namespace called database-ns, your backend would need to call it using mysql-service.database-ns.svc.cluster.local.

Isolation: Namespaces allow you to have a "Production" and "Development" environment in the same cluster without their services getting mixed up.
